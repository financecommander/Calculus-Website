The Case for Sound Money

Decades of monetary expansion, currency debasement, and inflationary policy have eroded purchasing power globally. Individuals, institutions, and sovereign entities increasingly seek alternatives that preserve value over time and resist arbitrary devaluation.

Constitutional Tender bridges the gap between time-tested sound money principles and modern digital infrastructure. By creating gold-backed and commodity-backed digital instruments, we provide a path toward monetary sovereignty in the digital age.

Rooted in constitutional principles of honest money and backed by real-world commodities, our solutions offer stability, transparency, and verifiable reserves — an antidote to the uncertainties of unbacked fiat systems.

Overview

What Is Constitutional Tender?

Constitutional Tender is a sound money and stable asset company backed by Calculus's private equity portfolio, applying constitutional monetary principles to the digital age. The company develops commodity-backed digital instruments — including gold-backed and precious metal-backed tokens — that provide an alternative to fiat currency and traditional stablecoins. Constitutional Tender's approach is rooted in Article I, Section 10 of the U.S. Constitution, which originally mandated gold and silver as legal tender. The company operates from Providence, Rhode Island, as part of Calculus's mission-driven portfolio.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Constitutional Tender?
Constitutional Tender is a company developing gold-backed and commodity-backed digital instruments based on sound money principles from the U.S. Constitution. It is a private equity portfolio company of Calculus, headquartered in Providence, Rhode Island, applying Article I, Section 10 constitutional monetary principles to the digital age.
What does Article I, Section 10 say about money?
Article I, Section 10 of the U.S. Constitution states that no state shall make anything but gold and silver coin a tender in payment of debts, establishing the constitutional basis for commodity-backed money. Constitutional Tender applies this principle to modern digital financial instruments.
How do gold-backed digital instruments work?
Each digital token is backed by a specific quantity of physical gold or precious metals held in secure custody, combining digital convenience with commodity backing. This provides an alternative to fiat currency and traditional algorithmic stablecoins through verifiable, real-asset reserves.
Is Constitutional Tender part of Calculus?
Yes, Constitutional Tender is a private equity portfolio company of Calculus, based in Providence, RI, and part of the firm's financial infrastructure investments. It reflects Calculus's commitment to building sound money solutions for the digital age.
