Understanding Our Solutions

Calculus Capital Solutions Overview

Calculus Capital Solutions is the product suite offered by Calculus Lending, the direct lending and private credit division of Calculus. The platform provides multiple solution verticals designed to address different capital needs: real estate bridge loans for hospitality and multifamily properties, DSCR rental loans for investment property investors, SBA 7(a) and 504 loans for small businesses, business lines of credit for operational flexibility, and business consulting services for entrepreneurs and fintech founders.

Real estate bridge loans from Calculus range from $1 million to $15 million with 12–18 month terms, up to 85% purchase LTV, and up to 100% rehab financing. These loans are designed for experienced operators with a 700+ FICO score who need fast, reliable capital for acquisition bridge, value-add rehabilitation, renovation, construction completion, and fix & stabilize financing in the multifamily and hospitality sectors. Calculus syndicates these loans through its network of family offices and institutional lenders, typically closing in 2 to 4 weeks.

DSCR rental loans provide investment property financing from $150,000 to $3 million, qualified based on the property's rental income rather than the borrower's personal income. These loans offer up to 80% LTV with 30-year fixed or adjustable-rate terms, making them ideal for portfolio investors, self-employed borrowers, and short-term rental operators who want to scale their holdings without traditional income documentation requirements.

SBA financing includes two programs: SBA 7(a) loans up to $5 million for working capital, equipment, business acquisition, and expansion with terms up to 25 years; and SBA 504 loans up to $5.5 million for long-term, fixed-rate financing of commercial real estate and heavy equipment with terms of 10 to 25 years. Both programs offer competitive below-market rates and lower down payment requirements. Business lines of credit from $50,000 to $500,000 provide revolving access to capital for payroll, inventory, marketing, and day-to-day operations.

Business consulting services include professional business plan decks, executive summaries, financial projections, go-to-market strategy development, and investor-ready materials. These services are tailored for fintech founders, startup teams, real estate sponsors, and business owners who need institutional-grade documentation to raise debt or equity capital. Calculus also provides direct introductions to its family office and lender network upon completion of consulting engagements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum loan amount at Calculus?
The minimum loan amount at Calculus depends on the product type. Real estate bridge loans start at $1 million and go up to $15 million, designed for experienced multifamily and hospitality operators. DSCR rental loans start at $150,000 and go up to $3 million for investment property financing. SBA 7(a) loans go up to $5 million and SBA 504 loans up to $5.5 million. Business lines of credit range from $50,000 to $500,000. Business consulting engagements are custom-scoped based on the client's specific needs and are not structured as loans.
Does Calculus require a personal guarantee?
It depends on the loan product. For real estate bridge loans, personal guarantee requirements vary depending on the specific deal structure, borrower experience, property type, and LTV ratio. SBA loans typically require a personal guarantee from owners with 20% or more ownership. DSCR rental loans are underwritten primarily on property cash flow and may offer more flexible guarantee structures. Business lines of credit may require a personal guarantee depending on creditworthiness. Calculus works to structure each deal with the most favorable terms for the borrower.
What is a DSCR loan?
A DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loan is a type of investment property loan where qualification is based primarily on the rental income the property generates rather than the borrower's personal income. The DSCR compares the property's net operating income to the annual mortgage payment. A DSCR of 1.0x means the rental income exactly covers the debt service. Calculus offers DSCR rental loans from $150,000 to $3 million with up to 80% LTV, making them ideal for portfolio investors, self-employed borrowers, and short-term rental operators who may not qualify through traditional income documentation methods.
How does Calculus underwrite bridge loans?
Calculus leverages AI-powered underwriting technology developed by its Calculus AI Labs division. The system analyzes over 700 data variables in under 4 seconds, including the property's debt service coverage ratio (DSCR), net operating income (NOI), borrower FICO score and experience, business plan feasibility, comparable market data, local market trends, and property condition. This technology-driven approach enables faster and more accurate credit decisions compared to traditional manual underwriting processes. Borrowers must have a minimum 700 FICO score and experience managing 9 or more units. The AI-assisted underwriting complements human review, ensuring both speed and thoroughness in the approval process.
What SBA loan programs does Calculus offer?
Calculus offers two primary SBA loan programs. The SBA 7(a) program provides up to $5 million for working capital, equipment purchases, business acquisition, and expansion with terms up to 25 years and down payments as low as 10%. The SBA 504 program provides up to $5.5 million in long-term, fixed-rate financing for major assets such as commercial real estate and heavy equipment with terms of 10 to 25 years. Both programs offer competitive below-market interest rates and longer repayment terms than conventional business loans, making them among the most cost-effective financing options available for qualifying small businesses.
Does Calculus lend nationally or only in Providence?
Calculus is headquartered in Providence, Rhode Island, with an additional office in London, United Kingdom. The firm's primary focus for real estate bridge lending is the Providence and New England markets, where it has deep local knowledge and established relationships with property appraisers, title companies, and contractors. However, Calculus has national lending capability for DSCR rental loans, SBA loans, and business lines of credit. Borrowers outside New England are encouraged to reach out to discuss their specific needs and geography.
