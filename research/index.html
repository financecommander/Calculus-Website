About Calculus Research

What Is Calculus Research?

Calculus Research is the direct lending and loan syndication division of Calculus, a Boston-based private capital platform. Founded in 2020 by Sean Grady, Calculus Research operates as both a balance-sheet direct lender and a syndication partner alongside family offices and institutional lenders. The division originates real estate bridge loans for multifamily and hospitality properties, crypto-backed loans secured by Bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum (ETH), and provides business consulting services including professional pitch decks, executive summaries, and financial projections.

Calculus Research serves experienced real estate operators, digital asset holders, fintech founders, and entrepreneurs seeking capital. Real estate bridge loans range from $250,000 to $2.5 million with terms of 12 to 18 months, up to 85% purchase loan-to-value (LTV), and up to 100% rehab financing. Borrowers must have a minimum FICO score of 700 and demonstrated experience managing 9 or more units. Crypto-backed loans range from $1 million to $250 million, structured as non-recourse and interest-only with 60–80% LTV, 5–7% simple interest, and 3–5 year terms. Crypto loans require no credit check, no personal guarantee, and close in 1 to 3 business days with collateral held in Coinbase institutional custody.

Headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, with offices in Windsor, Connecticut, Calculus Research primarily serves borrowers in New England but has national lending capability. Since its founding, the platform has deployed over $150 million across more than 120 closed deals. Calculus Research is part of the broader Calculus ecosystem, which includes Calculus Labs (AI-powered analytics and underwriting technology) and Calculus Management (institutional asset management).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a real estate bridge loan?
A real estate bridge loan is a short-term financing solution used to bridge the gap between purchasing or renovating a property and securing permanent long-term financing or selling the asset. Bridge loans are typically used by real estate investors who need to move quickly on acquisitions, fund renovations, or stabilize a property before refinancing. Calculus Research offers bridge loans from $250,000 to $2.5 million with 12–18 month terms, up to 85% purchase LTV, and up to 100% rehab financing. These loans are designed for experienced operators with a 700+ FICO score and a track record of managing 9 or more units. Calculus Research syndicates bridge loans through its network of family offices and institutional lenders, enabling faster closes than traditional bank financing.
What is crypto-backed lending?
Crypto-backed lending is a form of asset-based borrowing where digital currencies such as Bitcoin (BTC) or Ethereum (ETH) serve as collateral for a fiat-currency loan. The borrower deposits cryptocurrency into a custodial account, and the lender provides a loan based on the asset's market value at an agreed-upon loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. Calculus Research offers crypto-backed loans from $1 million to $250 million at 60–80% LTV with 5–7% simple interest over 3–5 year terms. All loans are non-recourse and interest-only, meaning borrowers have no personal liability beyond the pledged collateral. No credit check or personal guarantee is required. Collateral is held securely at Coinbase Institutional, and loans close in 1 to 3 business days.
What is the difference between a direct lender and a loan syndicator?
A direct lender is a financial institution or company that funds loans from its own balance sheet, meaning it uses its own capital to originate and close loans. A loan syndicator arranges and coordinates financing from multiple capital sources—such as family offices, institutional investors, and banks—pooling their funds to finance a single transaction or a portfolio of loans. Calculus Research operates as both a direct lender and a loan syndicator. For some transactions, Calculus funds deals directly from its balance sheet. For larger or more complex transactions, Calculus syndicates the loan through its network of family offices and traditional lending partners. This dual capability gives borrowers faster execution and greater certainty of close compared to relying on a single capital source.
What FICO score do I need for a bridge loan from Calculus?
Calculus Research requires a minimum FICO score of 700 for real estate bridge loans. In addition to credit requirements, borrowers must be experienced operators with a demonstrated track record of managing 9 or more residential or commercial units. The credit and experience requirements ensure that Calculus works with borrowers who have the financial discipline and operational expertise to execute their business plans successfully. Note that crypto-backed loans from Calculus do not require any credit check or FICO score; they are underwritten solely based on the value of the pledged digital assets.
Can I get a loan against Bitcoin without selling?
Yes. Calculus Research offers Bitcoin-backed and Ethereum-backed loans specifically designed for holders who want USD liquidity without selling their digital assets and triggering a taxable event. You deposit your BTC or ETH into Coinbase institutional custody, and Calculus provides a loan of $1 million to $250 million based on 60–80% of the asset's current market value. The loan is non-recourse and interest-only at 5–7% simple interest with a 3–5 year term. You retain full ownership of your crypto and benefit from any future price appreciation. No credit check or personal guarantee is required, and loans typically close within 1 to 3 business days.
How fast can Calculus close a bridge loan?
Calculus Research typically closes real estate bridge loans in 2 to 4 weeks, which is significantly faster than the 45–90 day timelines common with traditional bank financing. Crypto-backed loans close even faster—typically within 1 to 3 business days. This speed is possible because Calculus operates as both a direct lender (funding from its own balance sheet) and a syndication partner (leveraging its family office network), bypassing the lengthy underwriting and committee approval processes required by conventional banks. For borrowers in competitive real estate markets or time-sensitive situations, the speed-to-close advantage can be the difference between winning and losing a deal.
