Program Overview

What Is the USDA B&I Loan Program?

The USDA Business & Industry (B&I) Guaranteed Loan Program is one of the most powerful rural financing tools in the federal government’s lending portfolio — providing long-term, low-cost capital to businesses that create jobs in rural America.

Federal Guarantee

USDA guarantees up to 80% of the loan, reducing lender risk and enabling borrowers to access longer terms, lower rates, and higher leverage than conventional financing.

Rural Job Creation

The program prioritizes businesses that create or retain employment in rural communities with populations under 50,000 — supporting economic development where it matters most.

Flexible Use of Proceeds

Funds can be used for business acquisition, expansion, equipment, real estate, working capital, debt refinancing, and renewable energy — one of the most versatile federal loan programs available.

What Can B&I Loans Finance?

The B&I program is one of the broadest federal loan programs — supporting everything from manufacturing expansions to renewable energy installations.

Manufacturing & Processing

Equipment purchases, facility construction or expansion, and modernization for food processing, advanced manufacturing, wood products, and value-added agriculture.

  • Production equipment & machinery
  • Facility construction & expansion
  • Value-added agricultural processing

Renewable Energy

Biomass plants, solar installations, wind projects, and energy efficiency improvements. B&I loans layer with ITC, PTC, and USDA REAP grants for optimized capital stacks.

  • Biomass & wood-fired generation
  • Solar & wind installations
  • ITC / PTC / REAP stacking

Business Acquisition

Acquire an existing rural business, franchise, or operating company. B&I financing provides long-term acquisition capital with favorable amortization and lower equity requirements.

  • Full business acquisitions
  • Franchise purchases
  • Change of ownership financing

Commercial Real Estate

Purchase, construct, or improve commercial real estate for business operations. Up to 30-year terms with fixed or variable rates and below-market pricing through the federal guarantee.

  • Purchase or new construction
  • Renovation & improvements
  • Up to 30-year amortization

Healthcare & Community

Essential community facilities including healthcare clinics, senior care, childcare centers, and community services that support rural populations and local employment.

  • Rural healthcare facilities
  • Senior & assisted living
  • Community infrastructure

Debt Refinancing

Refinance existing debt when it improves cash flow and supports business viability. B&I refinancing replaces higher-cost conventional debt with longer terms and lower rates.

  • Conventional debt replacement
  • Cash flow improvement
  • Term extension & rate reduction

Overview

About the USDA B&I Loan Program

The USDA Business & Industry (B&I) Guaranteed Loan Program is administered by USDA Rural Development under 7 CFR Part 4279. It is the largest federal rural business loan program by dollar volume, designed to improve, develop, or finance business, industry, and employment in rural communities across the United States. The program provides loan guarantees to approved lenders, reducing credit risk and enabling access to capital for borrowers who may not qualify for conventional financing on comparable terms.

B&I loans range from $200,000 to $25 million with federal guarantee levels of 80% for loans up to $5 million, 70% for loans between $5 million and $10 million, and 60% for loans exceeding $10 million. Terms extend up to 30 years for real estate, 15 years for equipment and machinery, and 7 years for working capital. Interest rates are negotiated between the lender and borrower, typically benchmarked to the Wall Street Journal Prime rate. The guarantee fee structure includes a 3% initial fee on the guaranteed portion and a 0.5% annual renewal fee.

Eligible borrowers include for-profit businesses, nonprofit corporations, cooperatives, federally recognized Native American tribes, and public bodies. The business must be located in a rural area defined as any area with a population of 50,000 or less that is not an urbanized area adjacent to a city of 200,000 or more. Priority sectors include manufacturing, food processing, value-added agriculture, renewable energy, healthcare, and essential community infrastructure. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a USDA B&I loan?

A USDA Business & Industry (B&I) loan is a federally guaranteed loan program administered by USDA Rural Development. The program provides up to 80% loan guarantees to approved lenders, enabling them to offer favorable terms to rural businesses. B&I loans can be used for business acquisition, expansion, equipment, working capital, real estate, and renewable energy projects in eligible rural areas with populations under 50,000.

How much can you borrow with a USDA B&I loan?

USDA B&I loans range from $200,000 to $25 million. Guarantee percentages are 80% for loans up to $5 million, 70% for loans between $5 million and $10 million, and 60% for loans above $10 million. Terms extend up to 30 years for real estate, 15 years for equipment and machinery, and 7 years for working capital. Interest rates are negotiated between the lender and borrower, typically based on the Wall Street Journal Prime rate.

What businesses are eligible for USDA B&I loans?

Eligible borrowers include for-profit businesses, nonprofits, cooperatives, federally recognized tribes, and public bodies located in rural areas with populations under 50,000. Priority sectors include manufacturing, renewable energy, value-added agriculture, healthcare, food processing, and essential community services. The business must create or retain jobs in the rural community.

Can USDA B&I loans be used for renewable energy projects?

Yes. USDA B&I loans are widely used for renewable energy projects including biomass plants, solar installations, wind farms, and energy efficiency improvements. The program supports rural energy infrastructure and can be combined with other incentives such as the Investment Tax Credit (ITC), Production Tax Credit (PTC), and USDA REAP grants for layered capital structures.
