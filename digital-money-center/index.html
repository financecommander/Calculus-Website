Inclusive digital financial infrastructure — banking the unbanked through digital-first financial services that reach communities traditional banking cannot.

The Financial Inclusion Gap

Nearly 1.4 billion adults worldwide remain unbanked, lacking access to even basic financial services. Traditional banking infrastructure is expensive to build, slow to scale, and often excludes the populations that need it most.

Digital Money Center is building the infrastructure to close this gap. Through mobile-first digital financial services, we deliver banking, payments, savings, and credit to underserved communities — meeting people where they already are: on their phones.

Our mission is aligned with Calculus's core thesis: deploying capital where it creates the most impact, while generating sustainable, long-term returns for investors.

Mobile Money Platforms

Feature-phone and smartphone-native financial services enabling deposits, withdrawals, peer-to-peer transfers, and bill payments without a bank account.

Digital Wallets

Secure digital wallets with multi-currency support, micro-savings products, and instant merchant payments for the everyday economy.

Remittance Corridors

Low-cost cross-border remittance infrastructure connecting diaspora communities with their families, reducing fees from industry averages of 6-7% to under 2%.

Overview

What Is Digital Money Center?

Digital Money Center is a financial inclusion fintech company backed by Calculus's private equity portfolio, building digital financial infrastructure for the world's unbanked and underbanked populations. Through mobile money platforms, digital wallets, and cross-border remittance corridors, Digital Money Center enables access to savings, payments, and credit services for communities historically excluded from the traditional banking system. The company operates as part of Calculus's mission-driven impact portfolio, headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, with a focus on emerging markets across Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and Latin America.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Digital Money Center?
Digital Money Center is a fintech company building mobile money and digital wallet infrastructure for unbanked populations globally. It is a portfolio company in Calculus's private equity division, headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, with a focus on emerging markets across Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and Latin America.
How many people are unbanked worldwide?
Approximately 1.4 billion people globally lack access to basic financial services, primarily in Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and Latin America. Digital Money Center builds digital financial infrastructure to serve these underserved populations through mobile money platforms, digital wallets, and remittance corridors.
What services does Digital Money Center provide?
Digital Money Center provides mobile money platforms, digital wallets, cross-border remittance corridors, savings access, payment processing, and micro-credit services for communities historically excluded from the traditional banking system.
Is Digital Money Center part of Calculus?
Yes, Digital Money Center is a portfolio company in Calculus's private equity division, reflecting the firm's mission to drive financial inclusion through impact-aligned investments headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts.
